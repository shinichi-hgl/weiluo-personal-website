Designing Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence

Exploring the intersection of deep learning, cognitive science, and human-computer interaction.

I am an Assistant Professor of Computer Science. My research focuses on building AI systems that cooperate with humans, adapt to their workflows, and respect societal values. Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at MIT and received my Ph.D. from Carnegie Mellon University.

Feel free to look around my research work or reach out for collaboration opportunities.

Research Focus

My work sits at the intersection of AI models and human collaboration. I am particularly interested in designing algorithms that understand human intent, reason about social context, and present complex model explanations transparently.

Currently, my lab works on three core themes:

  • Interactive & Cooperative Learning: Designing RL systems that actively elicit feedback from domain experts to refine decision boundaries.
  • Interpretability & Explanations: Developing visualization frameworks that represent high-dimensional model states in intuitive 2D environments.
  • Social & Ethical AI Policies: Assessing bias propagation in large language models across multiple social groups and designing mitigation strategies.

Selected Publications

Journal 2026

Eliciting Human Intent in Interactive Reinforcement Learning

Sarah Jenkins, Michael H. Chang, Emily R. Robinson
IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence (TPAMI), 2026.
PDF Code
We present a novel paradigm for interactive reinforcement learning where agents actively query human experts during critical decision states. Our methodology leverages uncertainty estimation over the policy distribution to identify state spaces where human feedback is most valuable. We demonstrate a 40% reduction in sample complexity across standard human-AI cooperative benchmarks.
Conference 2025

Understanding Explainable AI: User Perception of Saliency Maps

Emily R. Robinson, Sarah Jenkins, David K. Wu
Proceedings of the ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI), 2025.
PDF Slides
This study conducts a large-scale user evaluation to measure how clinicians interpret model decisions using various explanation paradigms. Surprisingly, our results show that traditional saliency maps frequently mislead users in high-stakes scenarios. We propose an alternative visual explanation format based on counterfactual examples that substantially increases user decision accuracy.
Conference 2024

Mitigating Biased Associations in Multimodal Foundation Models

Sarah Jenkins, Kevin L. Miller, Marcus Thompson
International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2024.
PDF Code
Multimodal models mapping images and text together often reproduce and amplify gender and racial stereotypes. In this work, we present a projection technique to scrub sensitive attributes from cross-modal embedding spaces without altering semantic representation quality. Experiments on CLIP demonstrate significant fairness improvements.
